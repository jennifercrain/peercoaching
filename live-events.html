Peer Coaching Live Events

Group coaching support through strategy application and guided practice

What Are Live Events?

Peer Coaching Live Events provide group coaching support designed to help students engage with challenging course material through strategy application, guided practice, and reflection. Unlike appointments, Live Events are proactively planned using course data and trends to identify the areas where students may need the most support.

The AFFIRM Framework

Achievement Orientation & Frame the Session

Welcome students and build rapport by introducing the session topic, sharing a brief overview or agenda, and establishing the purpose of the session.

Frame a challenge within the course material as an observation:

  • "When I went through this course, I noticed…"
  • "When I look at this section, what sticks out is…"

Focus on the Strategy

Introduce a strategy designed to address the challenge identified during the introduction. Clearly explain what the strategy is, why it's helpful, and how students can use it effectively.

Model the strategy through a brief "I do" demonstration with a think-aloud, showing:

  • What you notice in the material
  • How you process information
  • How you organize or apply that information

Intentional Practice

Shift into the role of facilitator and guide students through actively applying the strategy themselves. Use powerful questions and engagement techniques to encourage participation.

Example questions:

  • "What do we notice about this text?"
  • "What stands out as most important?"
  • "How can we put this in our own words?"

Reflect and Move Forward

Prompt students to reflect on what was most impactful and identify next steps toward their goals.

Example questions:

  • "What is one takeaway you're leaving with tonight?"
  • "What strategy from tonight will help you most moving forward?"

Before closing, encourage students to attend future Live Events, connect through WGU Connect, and complete the feedback form.
