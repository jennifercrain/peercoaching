Peer Coaching Live Events

Group coaching support designed to help students engage with challenging course material

What Are Live Events?

Peer Coaching Live Events provide group coaching support designed to help students engage with challenging course material through strategy application, guided practice, and reflection. Unlike appointments, Live Events are proactively planned using course data and trends to identify the areas where students may need the most support.

The AFFIRM Framework

Achievement Orientation & Frame the Session

Peer Coaches begin by welcoming students into the room and building rapport. Early engagement helps students feel more comfortable participating throughout the session.

At the start of the session:

  • Introduce the session topic
  • Share a brief high-level overview or agenda
  • Establish the purpose of the session

Frame a challenge within the course material as an observation from your own experience, common trends, or the material's structure.

Examples:

  • "When I went through this course, I noticed…"
  • "When I look at this section, what sticks out is…"

Focus on the Strategy

The Peer Coach introduces a strategy designed to address the challenge identified during the introduction.

Clearly and concisely explain:

  • What the strategy is
  • Why it is helpful for this section of the course
  • How students can use it effectively

Model the strategy through a brief demonstration using an "I do" approach paired with a think-aloud, showing:

  • What you notice in the material
  • How you process information
  • How you organize or apply that information within the strategy

Intentional Practice

During Intentional Practice, the Peer Coach shifts into the role of facilitator and guides students through actively applying the strategy themselves.

Use powerful questions and engagement techniques to encourage participation and help students process information collaboratively.

Example questions:

  • "What do we notice about this text?"
  • "What stands out as most important?"
  • "Where does it make sense to put this information?"
  • "How can we put this in our own words or make this easier to remember?"

Reflect and Move Forward

Toward the end of the session, prompt students to reflect on what was most impactful and identify what next steps they will take toward their goals.

Example reflection questions:

  • "What is one takeaway you're leaving with tonight?"
  • "What is one thing you plan to do after this session?"
  • "What strategy from tonight do you think will help you most moving forward?"

Before closing the session, encourage students to:

  • Attend future Live Events
  • Connect with Peer Coaching through WGU Connect
  • Complete the provided feedback form
