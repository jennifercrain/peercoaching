Supporting Students in Crisis

WGU serves students from all walks of life and a wide range of life circumstances. Because of this, Peer Coaches may occasionally interact with students who are facing situations that require more support than a Peer Coach is able to provide.

While Peer Coaches play an important role in student success, it is important to remember that Peer Coaches are not counselors or therapists. The Peer Coach role is focused on providing strategies, structure, and resources to support academic challenges.

Important: Students' personal challenges can and do impact their academic journey; however, Peer Coaches are not the appropriate or qualified professionals to support personal, medical, or mental health concerns. When students share challenges outside academic scope, it's crucial to recognize this and provide appropriate resources.
