Digital Accessibility

Creating inclusive, accessible student-facing resources for all learners

What is Digital Accessibility?

Digital accessibility is a required expectation for all student-facing resources. Any materials created, presented, or shared with students should be designed in a way that supports readability, usability, and accessibility for all learners.

Email Accessibility Standards

📌 WGU Branding Requirement

Use Arial font, size 14 in all email interactions. Consistent formatting improves readability and supports accessibility across devices and assistive technologies.

⚙️ How to Update Outlook

  1. 1. Select Settings (cog wheel) in upper-right corner
  2. 2. Scroll down and select Mail
  3. 3. Select Compose
  4. 4. Set font to Arial, size 14

Templates & Presentations

Use Accessible Templates Whenever Possible

Accessibility-compliant templates are available for most study strategies and Canva presentations. These templates should be your first choice.

When might you need to adjust?

  • A template is not available
  • Adjustments are needed for presentation flow or delivery
  • Additional materials need to be created

Accessibility Guidelines

①

Use High Contrast

Text should clearly stand out from the background.

  • Use dark text on light backgrounds or light text on dark backgrounds
  • Avoid low-contrast color combinations
  • Do not rely on color alone to communicate meaning
②

Use Sans Serif Fonts

Sans serif fonts are easier to read in digital formats.

Arial Calibri Aptos Verdana
③

Use Readable Capitalization

Use clear, consistent capitalization in headings and body text.

✓ Preferred: "Time Management Strategies"

✗ Avoid: "TIME MANAGEMENT STRATEGIES"

④

Avoid Flashing or Distracting Visuals

Do not use flashing imagery, rapid animations, or distracting visual effects. Simple transitions and minimal animations are preferred.

⑤

Use Readable Spacing

Content should be easy to scan and follow.

  • Use appropriate line spacing
  • Leave spacing between sections and paragraphs
  • Avoid overcrowding slides with large amounts of text
⑥

Keep Slides and Visuals Simple

Presentations should support student learning without overwhelming the screen.

  • Use clear headings
  • Limit unnecessary text
  • Organize information logically
  • Use visuals that support the topic being discussed

⚠️ Accessibility Expectations

Accessibility is part of creating an inclusive and supportive coaching experience. All Peer Coaches are expected to apply accessibility best practices when creating, editing, or sharing student-facing materials.

Failure to comply with accessibility expectations may result in the loss of permission to create or customize additional templates.
