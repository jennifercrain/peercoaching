Programming Labs

In many courses, traditional study strategies work well for helping students organize ideas and process information. Programming can feel different. When students are working inside platforms like zyBooks or another coding environment, the challenge often is not studying the material itself. The challenge is figuring out what to do when code does not behave the way they expected. In those moments, students are already in the middle of the task. They are trying to run code, interpret results, and understand what went wrong. Debugging is a real-time thinking process.

When Something Breaks

When something breaks, students often begin reacting quickly instead of thinking through the situation. You may see students:

  • Change several things at once
  • Run the program repeatedly without predicting what will happen
  • Skip reading the full error message
  • Lose track of what they have already tested

This suggests the issue is often not a study strategy problem, but a process problem.

Programming Labs exist to support students during these moments. These sessions give Peer Coaches a structured way to guide a student's thinking while they work through coding labs or assignments. Instead of focusing only on traditional study strategies, Programming Labs emphasize debugging literacy and intentional problem-solving.

The Role of the Peer Coach

Programming Labs still follow the philosophy of Peer Coaching. Peer Coaches are not teaching programming concepts, correcting syntax, rewriting code, or providing direct solutions. Instead, the role of the coach is to help students slow down, observe what is happening, and think through the problem step by step.

Even if you immediately notice what might be wrong in the code, your role is not to provide the quick fix. Your role is to support the student's thinking process.

Clarify the problem
Interpret what they are seeing
Test ideas intentionally
Reflect on what worked

This approach keeps sessions aligned with Peer Coaching's mission while still providing meaningful support in programming environments.

The Lab Owl Framework

To support Programming Lab sessions, we use a framework called Lab Owl. Lab Owl serves as a structured coaching guide that helps Peer Coaches facilitate debugging conversations without stepping into tutoring or direct instruction.

Programming problems often feel overwhelming because students do not know where to start. Lab Owl gives both the Peer Coach and the student a clear direction for the conversation. It helps sessions remain structured, student-driven, focused on skill development, and aligned with Peer Coaching's mission.

When you follow the Lab Owl process, you help students move from confusion to clarity while building stronger debugging habits. The framework slows down the student's process so they can clearly observe what is happening and make intentional decisions about what to test next.

Lab Owl Helps Students:

Identify where a problem begins
Observe what their program is doing
Isolate variables to test
Recognize patterns in outputs
Build independent debugging habits
