Redirecting Questions

Staying engaged while honoring role boundaries

What Redirecting Is (and Is Not)

Redirecting IS:

  • ✓ Remaining fully engaged with the student
  • ✓ Helping them make sense of their confusion
  • ✓ Coaching them on locating, interpreting, and using resources
  • ✓ Supporting them in forming clear questions

Redirecting IS NOT:

  • → Dismissing the question
  • → Avoiding the conversation
  • → Withdrawing support
  • → Treating the student as if they're in the wrong place

Deep Dive: Each Step

1

Step 1: Redirect to the Group (Live Events)

In live events, the first move is not to remove the question from the space, but to open it to shared thinking. This keeps the student supported and reduces isolation.

"The peer coach stays present, facilitating — not disappearing."

2

Step 2: Redirect to Course Materials and Resources

If the answer lives in the course, the peer coach does not say, "That's in the material," and stop. Instead, you help the student locate the relevant section, identify what connects to their question, and decide how to re-engage more effectively.

"You are still actively working with the student — just not supplying the content directly."

3

Step 3: Redirect to the Course Instructor (With Support)

When the question truly requires academic interpretation or confirmation, you help the student clarify their confusion, narrow the question, put it into clear language, and identify how they will reach out.

"The student leaves with a plan, not a brush-off."

The Core Principle

Peer coaches do not answer content questions.

Peer coaches also do not abandon content questions.

They stay engaged by validating the confusion, supporting the thinking process, guiding effective resource use, and coaching question formation. Redirecting is active, not passive.
