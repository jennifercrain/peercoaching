Understanding Our AI Boundary

Peer Coaching Policy

In Peer Coaching, we do not discuss, recommend, demonstrate, or engage with AI tools with students — period. This is not a gray area, and it is not a judgment call or "case-by-case" basis.

Peer Coaching exists to foster authentic student-to-student connection and skill development through human interaction. Introducing AI into coaching conversations undermines our goal by shifting learning away from the student's own thinking and problem-solving process and removing peer-to-peer connection. Simply put, AI is incompatible with our mission.

The role of the peer coach is to:

  • Support how students learn
  • Strengthen their skills and confidence
  • Preserve the integrity of their learning experience

AI, by its nature, replaces or shortcuts those processes. Because of that, AI use with students is antithetical to the purpose of Peer Coaching. This boundary protects students, academic integrity, and the credibility and continued existence of the peer coach role.

IMPORTANT:

As AI continues to evolve, you may periodically receive AI-related communications or updates from the university. These updates do not override our Peer Coaching policy. Your Peer Coaching leaders will keep you informed of any changes.

Why This Boundary Exists

Our policy directly aligns with WGU's academic integrity, privacy, and AI standards. As of February 2026, WGU policies make four expectations clear:

Course materials and assessments are confidential.

Student information must be protected.

AI is a tool, but it must be used responsibly to protect academic integrity.

AI must never replace a student's own thinking.

Failure to uphold WGU's standards may result in corrective action or sanctions in accordance with university policy and employment expectations.

What Must Never Be Entered into AI Tools

The following must not be copied, pasted, uploaded, or summarized in any public or unapproved AI system.

WGU Course Materials

  • × Task and assessment prompts
  • × Rubrics
  • × Templates
  • × Quizzes and pre-assessments
  • × Course videos or transcripts
  • × Course pages or instructional resources

Student Information

  • × Names
  • × Email addresses
  • × Student ID numbers
  • × Phone numbers
  • × Screenshots or recordings that show identifying information

A simple rule to remember: If it came from WGU or identifies a student, it does not go into AI -- period.

AI Outside of Coaching Sessions

While AI is not used with students, limited internal use is permitted for professional development exercises and administrative support (not completion).

AI May Be Used to:

  • ✓ Generate ideas or prompts: Find yourself struggling to ask good questions during a certain stage of AFFIRM? AI can help you come up with ideas that you can apply to your sessions later.
  • ✓ Practice coaching skills: Did you have an appointment or live event that didn't go well? Without putting in sensitive or protected information, have AI be your student and practice how you might have approached the call differently.
  • ✓ Check spelling and grammar: Have AI polish your thoughts in student-facing materials for better engagement.

AI May Not Be Used to:

  • × Generate WGU Connect Community posts: Community posts should be thoughtfully written by you and focused on genuine engagement.
  • × Generate or reply to student emails: Student emails must sound human, genuine, and personalized.
  • × Complete Session Documentation: Student notes and Discussion Maps must be written directly by you.

Peer Coaching is built on peer interaction, lived experience, and intentional communication.

AI may support your growth as a coach, but it must never replace you in the coaching relationship.

What This Means in Practice

In Peer Coaching sessions and in internal work, peer coaches do not:

✗ Initiate AI-based discussions

✗ Paste WGU course materials into AI tools

✗ Ask students to place WGU content into AI systems

✗ Store coaching session notes or student information in AI tools
