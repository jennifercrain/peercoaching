1:1 Appointments

Individualized support tailored to each student's unique challenges and goals

What Are 1:1 Appointments?

Peer Coaching appointments provide individualized support tailored to each student's unique challenges, goals, and learning process. While no two appointments will look exactly the same, successful sessions generally follow the same coaching flow.

Coach Responsibilities

✓

Verify the student at the start of the appointment

✓

Ask clarifying questions to help students articulate what they want to achieve

✓

Focus on strategy and resources rather than giving advice or teaching content

✓

Let students actively practice the strategy while you facilitate and guide

✓

Send a follow-up email with resources discussed and a scheduling link

The Coaching Flow

Achievement Orientation & Frame the Session

Begin by asking questions that help the student clarify what they want to achieve. Avoid making assumptions based on your own experiences.

Example questions:

  • "Tell me a little bit about how things are going."
  • "What are some things you've tried so far?"
  • "Which would be most impactful to focus on right now?"

Focus on the Strategy

Introduce a strategy or resource that may help the student move forward. Clearly explain what the strategy is, why it's helpful, and how to use it effectively.

Model the strategy using an "I do" approach. Share relevant course material, demonstrate a template from the Peer Coaching Toolbox, and apply the strategy while thinking aloud through the process.

Intentional Practice

Shift into the role of facilitator. The goal is for the student to actively practice the strategy themselves. Do not complete the work, provide answers, or teach course content.

Guiding questions:

  • "What stands out most in this section?"
  • "What keywords or details would fit best into our strategy?"
  • "Where might we look for additional clarity?"

Reflect and Review

As the appointment closes, encourage the student to reflect on their experience, identify key takeaways, and determine next steps.

Reflection questions:

  • "What part of today's session was most helpful?"
  • "What is your biggest takeaway from today?"
  • "What is one thing you plan to do next?"

Let the student know they will receive a follow-up email with resources discussed, copies of templates used, and a scheduling link for future appointments.
