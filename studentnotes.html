Student Notes

Documenting student interactions and maintaining accurate records

Student Notes are a critical part of Peer Coaching. Documenting student interactions helps them maintain academic activity and creates a record of engagement for their Program Mentor and Course Instructor.

Student Notes Requirements

▼

All student note communication must be clear, complete, professional, and timely.

⚠️ Critical Requirements

  • Following these requirements is non-negotiable
  • Regular audits will provide feedback on your documentation
  • Failure to maintain accurate records can result in immediate end to internship

The Four Pillars:

Clear

Clearly summarize your interaction so others can easily understand the main points.

Complete

Provide useful, relevant information including AFFIRM model description and next appointment date.

Professional

DO NOT document diagnoses, medical info, or personal details.

Instead of: "challenges with time management due to ADHD"
Say: "history of challenges with time management"

Timely

All documentation must be completed by end of shift the same day. Date cannot be changed.

Using the Student Note Form

▼

The Student Note Form uploads interactions into Salesforce, allowing all WGU faculty and staff to see what support each student has received.

📌 Why Accurate Documentation Matters

A student on a "retake plan" needs Peer Coach support before CI approval. Without your note in Salesforce, the CI cannot approve the retake. Inaccurate notes prevent informed decisions about student support.

Note Form Options:

Live Call and Follow-up Email to Student

Phone call via Genesys Cloud (including Webex alongside). This is chosen most of the time.

Video Call and Follow-up Email to Student

Only Webex (for students with poor cell connectivity).

Voicemail and Follow-up Email to Student

Used when student doesn't answer and you leave voicemail. All calls require student notes.

Voicemail Full/Follow-up Email to Student

When voicemail inbox is full or not set up. All calls require student notes.

Email Exchange with Student

Student emails outside normal appointment process. All exchanges must be documented.

Key Takeaways

▼
  • Every interaction must be documented — whether answered, voicemail, or email
  • Documentation same day — complete before end of shift
  • Use professional language — avoid diagnoses and personal details
  • Accurate notes support success — helps PMs and CIs make informed decisions
  • This is non-negotiable — essential to your role as Peer Coach
