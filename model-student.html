Week Three Training

Serving as a Model Student

Representing WGU and the Peer Coaching program with professionalism and integrity

Your Role as a Representative

As a Peer Coach, you serve as a representative of both Western Governors University and the Peer Coaching program. In every interaction with students, colleagues, and leadership, you reflect the values, professionalism, and standards of the organization.

Peer Coaches are expected to consistently demonstrate behaviors aligned with being a model student—someone who engages fully with the learning process, takes ownership of their progress, and uses available resources to move forward.

What Model Students Do

📚

Engage Thoroughly

Engage with all assigned course materials and learning resources with intention and focus.

🛠️

Use Available Resources

Actively use WGU tools, supports, and academic resources to support their progress.

🎯

Take Ownership

Take ownership of their learning experience and follow through on commitments.

💪

Show Persistence

Demonstrate persistence and a willingness to adjust strategies when needed.

✅

Focus on Solutions

Focus on solutions and next steps when challenges arise.

💬

Participate Constructively

Participate in constructive, forward-focused academic conversations.

Important: Being a model student does not mean avoiding difficulty. Instead, it means fully engaging with the learning process and using available resources to move forward when challenges occur.

Communication Expectations

✓ Do This

  • ✅ Maintain professionalism in all student interactions
  • ✅ Use inclusive, gender-neutral language when referring to students or groups
  • ✅ Keep conversations focused on support, progress, and actionable next steps
  • ✅ Represent WGU and the Peer Coaching program with integrity

✗ Avoid This

  • ❌ Negative or unproductive commentary about courses, processes, staff, faculty, or systems
  • ❌ Reinforcing discouraging narratives
  • ❌ Unprofessional communication with students or colleagues
  • ❌ Conversations that undermine student confidence or WGU's mission

Supporting Students Through Challenges

Students may experience frustration, setbacks, or uncertainty. In these moments, Peer Coaches help students move forward in a constructive way:

🤝

Acknowledge

Listen to the student's experience without escalating negative framing or dismissing their concerns.

✓

Validate

Affirm that challenges are part of the learning process while staying forward-focused and solution-oriented.

🎯

Help Identify Control

Guide students to recognize what they can control or adjust in their approach.

💡

Reframe Challenges

Position challenges as opportunities to build skills, persistence, and confidence.

💬 Example Mindset Shift:

Instead of: "This course is impossible."

Try: "This is challenging, and let's identify resources and strategies to help you work through it step by step."

💼 Professional Representation

As a Peer Coach, your words and actions reflect WGU and the Peer Coaching program. Consistent professionalism helps build trust, supports student success, and reinforces a positive learning environment.

Every interaction is an opportunity to model the behaviors we encourage students to develop: engagement, accountability, persistence, and a solution-oriented approach to learning.
