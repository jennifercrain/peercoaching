Outreach Calling

Outreach calling, sometimes referred to as care calling, is an opportunity to proactively connect with students, build rapport, and offer support before a student feels disengaged. Outreach calling is not scheduled by students and they may not be expecting a call. Because of this, Peer Coaches focus on creating a supportive, low-pressure conversation centered on curiosity, encouragement, and connection to helpful resources.

With outreach, Peer Coaches aim to:

  • Understand how the student is doing
  • Identify possible barriers or challenges
  • Connect the student to resources or strategies
  • Encourage continued engagement with their course and support systems

Beginning the Call

Peer Coaches initiate outreach calls through Genesys Cloud using the Outreach Call List located on the appropriate course SharePoint.

If the student does not answer:

Leave a brief, professional voicemail and follow up by email.

"Hi, this is [Name] from WGU. I'm going to send a follow-up email, and I hope we can reconnect another time. Thank you!"

If the student answers:

Introduce yourself, explain your role, and ask whether it is a good time to talk.

"Hi [Student Name]! This is [Your Name], and I'm a Peer Coach at WGU. I was hoping we could chat for a few minutes. Is now a good time?"

"No problem! I'll send a follow-up email in hopes that we can connect another time. Have a great day!"

Before discussing student information, verify the student's identity by requesting:

  • First and last name
  • Program Mentor's name
  • Student ID number (or WGU username if student doesn't have access to student ID)

Leading the Conversation

After verification, explain the purpose of the outreach call and begin exploring the student's current experience in the course. The goal is to help students reflect on their challenges and identify areas where support may be helpful or what they need to continue.

Example questions:

• "How is [Course] going for you?"

• "Is there anything in the course that's been feeling challenging lately?"

• "What's been the hardest thing to juggle with school right now?"

• "Has anything surprised you about how this course is going?"

• "What kind of support would make the biggest difference for you this week?"

Peer Coaches may briefly validate that a course can feel challenging while remaining focused on encouragement and problem-solving.

Connecting Students to Support

Once a challenge or concern has been identified, introduce a relevant strategy, resource, or support service that may help the student move forward.

Resources may include:

  • Course resources or cohorts
  • Academic Coaching Center articles or services
  • Live events
  • Peer coaching appointments
  • Program Mentors
  • Student Services
  • Student Wellbeing Services

Peer Coaches briefly explain:

  • What the resource is
  • How it may help
  • How the student can access it

If discussing a strategy, keep the conversation focused and approachable. Outreach calls are intended to introduce support and encourage engagement rather than provide in-depth coaching or lengthy instruction. If a student needs more detailed strategy support, encourage them to attend a live event or schedule an appointment for additional practice and guidance.

Inviting Students to Reconnect

Peer Coaches invite students to continue engaging with support services in a way that aligns with their role.

For Appointment-Based Peer Coaches:

Encourage students to schedule a future session for more individualized support.

"I also offer Peer Coaching appointments where we can walk through study strategies, explore resources together, or even just check in as accountability partners for this course."

"Would you be interested in scheduling time to reconnect?"

If the student is interested, attempt to schedule the appointment during the call.

For Live Event Coaches:

Personally invite students to an upcoming live event and explain how the event may help support the student's goals or challenges.

"My next Live Event is coming up on [Date and Time], and we'll be talking about [Topic]."

"We're going to focus on [Strategy] and how it can help make that section of the course easier to break down."

If the student cannot attend, provide a general appointment link or suggest alternative support options.

Closing the Call

Before ending the conversation, ask whether the student has any remaining questions or additional concerns.

"Before we wrap up, do you have any questions for me?"

"Is there anything else I can help support you with today?"

Close the call warmly and professionally while reinforcing continued support.

"Thank you so much for your time. It was really great talking with you."

"If you need anything, feel free to reply to my email and we'll make sure you get connected to the right resources."

Let students know they will receive a follow-up email containing:

  • Relevant resources
  • Scheduling links or live event information
  • Additional support information discussed during the call

Outreach Documentation and Procedures

After each outreach interaction, Peer Coaches complete the required documentation and follow-up tasks.

This includes:

  • Logging the call start and end time on the Outreach Call List
  • Sending the follow-up email
  • BCC'ing their Supervisor and Manager
  • Completing the Outreach-Specific Student Note Form
  • Tracking outreach interactions appropriately on the Interaction Data Form

Important: Outreach interactions should be logged as Outreach interactions, not Email Exchanges. Email Exchanges are reserved for transactional back-and-forth email communication with students.

All follow-up emails should be:

  • Properly personalized
  • Professionally formatted
  • Aligned with expectations
