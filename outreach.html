Outreach Calling

Outreach calling is an opportunity to proactively connect with students, build rapport, and offer support before a student feels disengaged. The goal is to understand how students are doing, identify barriers, connect them to resources, and encourage continued engagement.

Beginning the Call

Peer Coaches initiate outreach calls through Genesys Cloud using the Outreach Call List. If the student doesn't answer, leave a brief voicemail and follow up by email. If they answer, introduce yourself and ask if it's a good time to talk.

Before discussing student information, verify identity by requesting:

  • First and last name
  • Program Mentor's name
  • Student ID number (or WGU username)

"Hi [Student Name]! This is [Your Name], and I'm a Peer Coach at WGU. I was hoping we could chat for a few minutes. Is now a good time?"

Leading the Conversation

After verification, explain the purpose of the outreach call and explore the student's current experience. Focus on helping them reflect on challenges and identify where support may help.

• "How is [Course] going for you?"

• "Is there anything in the course that's been feeling challenging lately?"

• "What's been the hardest thing to juggle with school right now?"

• "What kind of support would make the biggest difference for you this week?"

Connecting Students to Support

Once a challenge is identified, introduce a relevant strategy, resource, or support service. Keep the conversation focused and approachable—outreach calls introduce support rather than provide in-depth coaching.

Resources may include:

  • Course resources or cohorts
  • Academic Coaching Center articles or services
  • Live events and peer coaching appointments
  • Program Mentors and Student Services
  • Student Wellbeing Services

Briefly explain what the resource is, how it may help, and how to access it.

Closing the Call

Before ending, ask if the student has remaining questions or concerns. Close warmly and professionally while reinforcing continued support.

• "Before we wrap up, do you have any questions for me?"

• "Thank you so much for your time. It was really great talking with you."

• "If you need anything, feel free to reply to my email and we'll make sure you get connected to the right resources."

Follow-up: Send a follow-up email containing relevant resources, scheduling links, and support information discussed. Log the call, complete documentation, and track interactions appropriately.
