Engagement Techniques

Create interaction, encourage participation, and keep students actively involved

What Are Engagement Techniques?

Engagement techniques help create interaction, encourage participation, and keep students actively involved during live sessions. While group discussion is important, Peer Coaches should vary engagement methods throughout the session rather than relying only on open discussion.

💡 Key Reminder: Always provide clear on-screen and verbal directions before asking students to participate.

Engagement Techniques

Group Discussion

What It Is

An open discussion where students participate verbally on mic or through the chat.

What the Peer Coach Should Do

  • Encourage students to join the conversation by unmuting or using the chat
  • Remind students they can use the hand raise feature if concerned about interrupting
  • Monitor participation and create opportunities for multiple students to contribute
  • Use follow-up questions and acknowledgements to keep discussion moving

Why It Works

Group discussion helps students feel connected to the session and encourages conversation between participants rather than only between the Peer Coach and students.

Popcorn Responses

What It Is

A fast-paced discussion technique where students all submit their responses at the same time in the chat.

What the Peer Coach Should Do

  • Ask students a question and have them type their response in the chat without pressing Enter yet
  • Once most students appear ready, give a clear signal such as "3…2…1…GO!" for everyone to submit at once
  • Review responses out loud and guide discussion based on common themes, differences, or strong examples

Why It Works

This technique encourages broad participation, prevents students from copying earlier answers, and creates energy and anticipation in the session.

Participant Reactions ("Emoji Voting")

What It Is

A quick engagement strategy where students use reactions or emojis to respond to prompts, vote, or show agreement.

What the Peer Coach Should Do

  • Show students where they can find the reaction feature in Webex
  • Ask a simple question or provide response options tied to specific emojis
  • Acknowledge the results verbally and use them to transition into discussion

Why It Works

Emoji voting provides a low-pressure way for students to participate and helps quickly gauge understanding, opinions, or energy levels.

Polls

What It Is

A structured way to collect student responses using a poll, survey, or chat-based question.

What the Peer Coach Should Do

  • Keep polls short and focused on one or two key questions
  • Use Webex polling tools or ask students to respond in the chat
  • Share the results with the group and facilitate discussion around the responses

Why It Works

Polls increase interaction and help spark discussion, especially when questions do not have a single correct answer.

Round Robin

What It Is

A discussion technique where students take turns sharing ideas verbally or in the chat.

What the Peer Coach Should Do

  • Explain participation expectations before beginning
  • Call on students one at a time to share their thoughts
  • Allow students the option to "pass" once if they are not ready to participate
  • Encourage respectful listening and acknowledge each response

Why It Works

Round Robin encourages balanced participation and ensures multiple voices are included in the conversation.
