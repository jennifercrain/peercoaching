Role Clarity and the Difference Between Coaching and Teaching

Why Role Clarity Matters

Students at WGU interact with several support roles, sometimes within the same day. Because those roles can feel similar, it's important for Peer Coaches to understand their role and how it differs from others—especially instructors.

The Two Key Roles

Course Instructors

Subject-matter experts (SMEs) whose role centers on what students learn and how it's evaluated.

They support students by:

•

Explaining course concepts and terminology

•

Clarifying task directions and rubric language

•

Interpreting assessment requirements

•

Confirming or correcting understanding

Peer Coaches

Academic support professionals whose role focuses on how students learn, not what they learn.

They focus on:

•

How the student approaches their learning

•

Planning, organization, and study strategies

•

Using strategies and resources effectively

•

Thinking through obstacles and next steps

Coaching vs. Teaching: Key Differences

Teaching and coaching serve different purposes. Understanding these differences helps you stay aligned with your role.

Teaching (Course Instructor Role)

Directive and content-focused

Explaining information

Clarifying meaning

Providing correct answers

Giving knowledge directly

Evaluating understanding

Coaching (Peer Coach Role)

Process-focused and student-centered

Asking powerful questions

Supporting analysis and reflection

Strategy selection and planning

Building self-efficacy and independence

Guiding, not telling

The goal of Peer Coaching is not to be the source of knowledge, but to help the student become more effective at accessing, organizing, and applying knowledge on their own.

Why Students Sometimes Confuse the Roles

Why Students Bring Content Questions to Peer Coaches:

1

They feel more comfortable talking to a peer

2

They are anxious and want quick confirmation

3

They are unsure who to contact

4

They don't yet distinguish between learning process support and instructional support
