Handling AI Situations

Practical Strategies for Coaching Conversations

Redirecting AI Conversations in Coaching Sessions

Peer Coaches use the same redirect skills with AI conversations that they use with content-specific questions. If a student brings up AI, do not engage in discussion about how to use it. Instead, redirect the conversation back to a learning strategy or resource, and skill development.

Important Clarification:

In limited circumstances, Course Instructors may provide AI-related guidance within their courses, taking authority over WGU's policies. If this occurs:

  • The Course Instructor assumes responsibility for that direction and supporting the student with that step.
  • Peer coaches do not interpret, expand on, or provide additional AI guidance.
  • Our role does not change. When unsure, follow Peer Coaching's policy and redirect.

Where Can I Find AI Resources for Students?

If students have questions about general AI policies, approved tools, or related topics, you may coach them through locating existing resources. Keep the focus on the process of finding information and how this approach supports students in addressing similar questions independently in the future.

Key Coaching Approach: Take care to prevent the conversation from shifting into detailed discussions about AI use, demonstrations of approved tools, or sharing personal opinions or experiences with AI.

Remember that coaching is not only about answering the immediate question but also about helping students develop repeatable processes for finding information independently. When locating resources, model the steps you are taking so students can follow and replicate them later. Avoid relying on personal bookmarks or sending links after the session; instead, demonstrate how to navigate to the information in real time.

Important: Just like with content, if the available materials don't answer a student's question, you are still responsible for trying to provide a coaching experience before you refer them back to their Course Instructor (or, in the case of policy questions, their Program Mentor is another option).
