Week Two Training

Study Strategies Matter

Learn how to help students build active learning skills

Why Study Strategies Matter

Study strategies are the tools students use to learn, remember, and make sense of their course material. They involve actively working with information so it becomes clearer, more organized, and easier to recall later.

Peer coaches focus on helping students learn how to use these strategies so they can take ownership of their learning. Peer coaches do not coach students on course content itself. Instead, any discussion of course material happens only through the lens of a study strategy. The strategy comes first, and the content is used only as the practice material for applying that strategy. This keeps the focus on building learning skills while supporting students as they use those skills to understand their coursework. Content is not discussed in the absence of a strategy being applied.

How Strategies Help Students Learn

When students use study strategies, they are not just reading or watching and hoping it sticks. They are actively doing something with the information. This helps them:

  • •Break large amounts of content into smaller, manageable parts
  • •Put ideas into their own words
  • •See how concepts connect to each other
  • •Organize information in a way that makes sense to them
  • •Remember what they studied when they need it on an assessment

Using strategies turns studying from something passive into something active. Instead of information just passing by, students are working with it, reshaping it, and checking their understanding.

Strategies Help Students Teach Themselves

When students apply a strategy, they are essentially teaching themselves the material. They are deciding what the main ideas are, how to explain them in their own words, and how to organize them so they can be understood later.

This process helps students:

  • •Build real understanding instead of simply memorizing (low order thinking)
  • •Notice and clarify what they do and do not understand
  • •Catch confusion earlier
  • •Make meaning out of complex or unfamiliar topics
  • •Become more confident and independent learners

Peer coaches do not teach course content, but they support students in learning how to learn. By using strategies, students take an active role in building their own understanding rather than relying on someone else to explain everything to them.

Why This Matters in Peer Coaching

Every course is different, but the skills students use to learn can carry across courses. When students learn how to use effective study strategies, they gain tools they can use again and again.

Study strategies help students:

  • •Learn more efficiently
  • •Study with purpose instead of guessing what to do
  • •Reduce overwhelm by having a clear approach
  • •Prepare more effectively for assessments
  • •Become more confident in their ability to figure things out

By focusing on strategies, peer coaches help students build skills that support not just one class, but their entire academic journey.

Exploring the Peer Coaching Toolbox

Peer coaches have access to more than 30 different study strategies, organized into areas like active recall, note-taking, graphic organizers, reading strategies, and more. These strategies are housed in the Peer Coaching Toolbox, where you can find templates, examples, and short demonstration videos that show what using each strategy can look like in practice.

Before moving on to the next quiz, you are encouraged to spend at least one hour exploring the toolbox. Take time to click through the different categories, review the strategy descriptions, look at the templates, and watch the demonstration videos. This will help you become familiar with the range of tools available and start building a sense of which strategies might fit different types of student challenges.
