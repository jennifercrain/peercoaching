Feedback and Growth

Embracing feedback as a foundation for continuous development

What to Expect During Your Growth

Internships are designed for development. They are an opportunity to build new skills, strengthen your practice, and grow both personally and professionally. Feedback is a key part of that process and will be provided regularly to support your continued development.

Feedback is intended to be constructive and respectful. It is centered on helping you refine your approach, build confidence, and develop into the strongest version of yourself in the Peer Coach role.

While training is designed to prepare you with essential skills to get started, it cannot cover every situation you may encounter. You may come across student interactions that feel unfamiliar or more challenging than what was covered in training. This is expected.

When this happens, your leaders will provide feedback and coaching to help you adjust and prepare for similar situations in the future. The goal is not perfection coming out of training, but steady growth as you gain experience in the role.

Growth comes from trying, learning, and applying what you discover to improve your coaching skills over time.

Having a Growth Mindset

Understanding the Difference

A fixed mindset tends to view feedback as criticism or a reflection of ability. In contrast, a growth mindset sees feedback as information that helps you improve over time.

In this role, development is expected and ongoing, and feedback is a normal and valuable part of that process.

Example:

❌ Fixed Mindset:

"I'm just not good at handling that type of student interaction."

✅ Growth Mindset:

"I haven't fully developed that skill yet, but I can improve it with practice and feedback."

Taking Feedback Seriously

There is an expectation that feedback is taken seriously and reasonably implemented. This does not mean perfection, but it does mean making a genuine effort to apply what is shared, adjust your approach, and show progress over time.

Reflection and Reflective Practice

An important part of developing in this role is taking time to reflect on your experiences. Reflection helps you connect feedback, training, and real student interactions so you can continue to grow with intention.

Reflective practice means thinking through what went well, what was challenging, and what you might adjust moving forward. It is not about being overly critical of yourself, but about building awareness and improving your approach over time.

💭 What Went Well?

Identify moments in a student interaction where you applied a strategy successfully or handled a situation with confidence.

🔍 What Was Challenging?

Notice areas where you felt uncertain or where you handled a situation differently than you might have hoped.

📝 What Would You Adjust?

Consider small changes or approaches that could improve similar situations in the future.

The Feedback & Reflection Cycle

Reflection is also closely tied to feedback. When feedback is provided, taking time to reflect on it helps ensure it is understood and applied in a practical way. Over time, this cycle of feedback and reflection supports steady growth and stronger performance in the role.
