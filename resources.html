Approved Materials & Support Services for Student Success

WGU Resources Only

In Peer Coaching, you may only use and share resources that are approved and provided by WGU. Resources that are not part of WGU's official materials should not be introduced into coaching sessions. This means you should not bring in outside websites, videos, or communities, such as Google search results, YouTube videos, resources made by other students (such as external study guides or Quizlets), or Discord channels.

Core Principle: Only WGU-approved resources in coaching sessions. This ensures consistency, quality, and alignment with institutional standards.

Course Resources

Demonstrating available course resources and how to use them is a key focus. It's important not only to highlight the resources but also to show students how to locate and apply them. This approach helps students become more resourceful and self-directed across all of their courses.

Pro Tip: Rather than simply screensharing a study guide, demonstrate how to access the WGU Connect community, navigate to the resource section, and open the materials. Remember, WGU Connect itself is a valuable resource!

Show How Resources Support Success

Consider the following questions when highlighting resources:

  • • Does this resource help students break down and organize content?
  • • Does it highlight areas where students may need additional focus?
  • • Does it present information differently from the course material, such as through visuals or simplified explanations?

Encourage Student Participation

Invite students to interact in simple ways, such as using emojis to indicate they've used a resource (in live events) or responding to powerful questions about how a specific type of resource has helped them previously. You can also foster engagement by asking broader questions, for example:

  • • "Would anyone like to share their experience with this study guide?"
  • • "How have study guides helped you in the past?"

Don't Overlook Hidden Resources

You might not initially think of items like Course Instructor cohorts, practice quizzes, or knowledge checks as "resources," but they absolutely are! Encourage students to give and reflect on their experiences.

  • Cohorts: Ask what insights they gained, what they learned, and how it helped their understanding.
  • Practice Quizzes & Learning Checks: Show students how to review all answer choices—even incorrect ones—to develop deeper understanding.

Key Resource Hubs

The Academic Coaching Center

Home to Peer Coaching, this hub offers a wealth of resource articles on study strategies, Performance Assessments, and Objective Assessments. Articles include templates, guides, how-to instructions, dos and don'ts, and assessment interpretation support.

Key Resources:

  • ✓ PA and OA support articles
  • ✓ Study strategy guides
  • ✓ Professional communication templates
  • ✓ Power Skills: time management, burnout, and more

Strategy: Consider what students find most challenging and their demographics. New first-term learners may need PA and similarity check guidance, while further-along students may benefit from Power Skills support.

The Student Handbook

Often overlooked and perceived as merely a list of rules, the Student Handbook is actually a comprehensive and valuable resource. It can answer many common student concerns about policies, procedures, and support.

Key Resources:

  • ✓ Being Successful with Online Proctoring
  • ✓ Academic integrity and policies
  • ✓ Support services and procedures
  • ✓ Searchable content for student concerns

Coaching Tip: Invite students to share a concern or worry they have, then practice using the search function together to find concrete answers. This alleviates fears while introducing them to resources they may not know about.

Student Wellbeing & Career Services

Remember that peer coaches are not counselors. When students experience personal challenges impacting their wellbeing or career goals, refer them to the appropriate WGU resource.

  • Student Wellbeing Services

    Provides free short-term counseling for mental health concerns, financial difficulties, relationship or home challenges, and more. Do not downplay its breadth—it's versatile and valuable.

  • Career & Professional Development Services

    Assists with resumes, career support, and Handshake (the platform for career opportunities and Peer Coach applications).

Important: Keep focus on relevance to the course while acknowledging broader applications. Connect these services back to students' course-related needs.
